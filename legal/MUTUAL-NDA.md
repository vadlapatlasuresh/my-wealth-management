**[INSERT COMPANY LEGAL NAME]**, a **[INSERT ENTITY TYPE, e.g., Delaware corporation]** with its principal place of business at **[INSERT ADDRESS]** ("**Company**"); and

## 1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

**1.1 Confidential Information.** "**Confidential Information**" means any and all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of a Disclosing Party to a Receiving Party, whether before or after the Effective Date, in any form or medium — whether written, oral, visual, electronic, or observed — and whether or not marked, designated, or otherwise identified as confidential.

**1.2 Included Categories.** Without limiting Section 1.1, Confidential Information expressly includes:

**(a) Technical and Architectural Information.** Financial algorithms, models, scoring methodologies, risk engines, rules engines, categorization and classification logic, reconciliation logic, and the mathematical or statistical methods underlying any of the foregoing; system and infrastructure architecture, including service decomposition, microservice boundaries, inter-service protocols, deployment topology, and network design; database schemas, data models, table structures, indices, and entity relationships; application programming interfaces (APIs), including endpoint definitions, request and response payloads, and authentication schemes; source code, object code, pseudocode, and code comments; software design documents, technical specifications, and architecture decision records.

**(b) Security Information.** Encryption schemes and key-management architecture; authentication and authorization design; audit-logging and integrity-verification mechanisms; security controls, threat models, penetration-test results, and known vulnerabilities. *(Note: Section 5.5 restricts disclosure of vulnerability information.)*

**(c) Trade Secrets.** Any information that constitutes a trade secret under the Defend Trade Secrets Act of 2018 (18 U.S.C. § 1836 et seq.) or under applicable state law, including the **[INSERT STATE]** Uniform Trade Secrets Act.

**(d) Product and Design Information.** Product roadmaps, feature specifications, unreleased functionality, user-interface and user-experience designs, wireframes, mockups, design systems, and prototypes.

**(e) Business Information.** Business models, monetization strategy, pricing and pricing methodology, unit economics, financial statements, projections, and forecasts; customer and user lists, customer data, and usage analytics; vendor, supplier, and partner identities, and the terms of agreements with them; marketing strategy, go-to-market plans, and market research; the identity of and terms discussed with prospective investors, employees, and acquirers.

**(f) Personal and Financial Data.** Any personally identifiable information, financial account information, transaction data, or consumer financial information disclosed in connection with the Purpose, including any information subject to the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act, applicable state financial-privacy laws, or applicable data-protection laws. *(Note: Section 12 addresses this category separately.)*

**(g) The Relationship Itself.** The existence and terms of this Agreement, the fact that discussions between the Parties are occurring, the Purpose, and the substance and status of those discussions.

**(h) Derivatives.** All notes, analyses, compilations, studies, summaries, extracts, models, and other materials prepared by or for a Receiving Party that contain, are based upon, or are derived from any of the foregoing.

**1.3 Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate, by contemporaneous written records:

(a) was lawfully in the Receiving Party's possession, without restriction on use or disclosure, before the Disclosing Party disclosed it;

(b) is or becomes publicly available through no act or omission of the Receiving Party or any of its Representatives in breach of this Agreement;

(c) was lawfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party that, to the Receiving Party's knowledge after reasonable inquiry, was not under any obligation of confidentiality with respect to it; or

(d) was independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of, reference to, or reliance upon any Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party, and without participation of any individual who had access to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information.

**1.4 Burden and Standard of Proof.** The Receiving Party bears the burden of establishing any exclusion under Section 1.3 by clear and convincing evidence. An exclusion applies only to the specific information falling within it; a general disclosure in the public domain does not place a specific item of Confidential Information in the public domain, and a combination of individually public elements is not excluded unless the combination itself, in its precise configuration, is public.

## 2. OBLIGATIONS OF CONFIDENTIALITY

**2.1 Non-Disclosure.** The Receiving Party shall hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not disclose it to any person or entity except as expressly permitted by this Agreement.

**2.2 Standard of Care.** The Receiving Party shall protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of like importance, and in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

**2.3 Permitted Recipients.** The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information only to those of its employees, officers, directors, contractors, and professional advisors (collectively, "**Representatives**") who (a) have a bona fide need to know it for the Purpose, and (b) are bound by written obligations of confidentiality and non-use at least as protective as this Agreement — or, in the case of attorneys and accountants, by professional duties of confidentiality.

**2.4 Responsibility for Representatives.** The Receiving Party is responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives, and any such breach shall be deemed a breach by the Receiving Party. The Receiving Party shall, at its own expense, take reasonable steps to restrain its Representatives from any unauthorized use or disclosure.

**2.5 Notice of Unauthorized Disclosure.** The Receiving Party shall notify the Disclosing Party in writing promptly, and in any event within **[INSERT NUMBER, e.g., three (3)]** business days, after becoming aware of any unauthorized use, disclosure, loss, or compromise of Confidential Information, and shall cooperate reasonably with the Disclosing Party to mitigate its effects.

**2.6 No Copies Beyond the Purpose.** The Receiving Party shall not copy, reproduce, or reduce to writing any Confidential Information except as reasonably necessary for the Purpose. All copies remain the property of the Disclosing Party and are subject to this Agreement.

---

## 3. NON-USE

**3.1 Use Limited to the Purpose.** The Receiving Party shall use the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information **solely** for the Purpose. The Receiving Party shall not use the Confidential Information for any other purpose whatsoever, including for its own benefit or the benefit of any third party.

**3.2 No Competing Development Using Confidential Information.** Without limiting Section 3.1, the Receiving Party shall not use, refer to, or rely upon any of the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information — directly or indirectly, alone or with others — to design, develop, build, specify, fund, or commercialize any product, service, or feature that competes with, or is substantially similar to, any product, service, or feature of the Disclosing Party disclosed to or observed by the Receiving Party under this Agreement.

**3.3 No Reverse Engineering.** The Receiving Party shall not reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or otherwise attempt to derive the source code, structure, architecture, algorithms, or underlying ideas of any software, prototype, system, or object code disclosed or made accessible to it, except to the extent this restriction is unenforceable under applicable law.

**3.4 No License Granted.** Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party any license or right, by implication, estoppel, or otherwise, under any patent, copyright, trade secret, trademark, or other intellectual property right of the Disclosing Party. All Confidential Information remains the exclusive property of the Disclosing Party.

**3.5 Scope Clarification.** Section 3.2 restricts use of Confidential Information. It does not restrict the Receiving Party from engaging in any business activity conducted without use of, reference to, or reliance upon the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information. **This Agreement is not a non-competition agreement and shall not be construed as one.**

## 5. COMPELLED DISCLOSURE AND STATUTORY CARVE-OUTS

**5.1 Legally Compelled Disclosure.** If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, subpoena, civil investigative demand, or other legal or regulatory process to disclose any Confidential Information, it may do so, provided that it shall (a) unless legally prohibited, give the Disclosing Party prompt written notice sufficient to allow the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order; (b) cooperate reasonably, at the Disclosing Party's expense, with any effort by the Disclosing Party to obtain such an order; and (c) disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that it is legally required to disclose, and use reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment for it.

**5.2 Regulatory Examination.** A Party that is a regulated financial institution or service provider may disclose Confidential Information to its primary regulators in the ordinary course of supervision or examination without prior notice, where notice is impracticable or prohibited.

**5.3 Defend Trade Secrets Act Notice.** Under 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b), an individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any federal or state trade-secret law for disclosing a trade secret that (a) is made in confidence to a federal, state, or local government official, directly or indirectly, or to an attorney, solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (b) is made in a complaint or other document filed under seal in a lawsuit or other proceeding. An individual who files a lawsuit for retaliation for reporting a suspected violation of law may disclose the trade secret to their attorney and use it in the court proceeding, if the individual files any document containing the trade secret under seal and does not disclose the trade secret except pursuant to court order.

**5.4 Protected Activity.** Nothing in this Agreement prohibits either Party or any individual from (a) reporting possible violations of law to, filing a charge with, or participating in an investigation by any federal, state, or local governmental agency or regulatory authority, including the Securities and Exchange Commission, the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority, the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission, the National Labor Relations Board, or the Department of Justice; (b) receiving any award for information provided to a governmental agency; (c) discussing or disclosing conduct the individual reasonably believes to be unlawful discrimination, harassment, retaliation, a wage-and-hour violation, or sexual assault, or conduct recognized as against a clear mandate of public policy; or (d) exercising rights under Section 7 of the National Labor Relations Act. No prior notice to or authorization from the other Party is required for any of the foregoing.

**5.5 Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure.** If a Receiving Party discovers a security vulnerability in the Disclosing Party's systems, it shall report it promptly to the Disclosing Party and shall not publicly disclose it for **[INSERT PERIOD, e.g., ninety (90)]** days or until the vulnerability is remediated, whichever is earlier. Nothing in this Section limits Sections 5.1, 5.3, or 5.4, or prohibits disclosure to a governmental agency at any time.

## 6. TERM AND SURVIVAL

**6.1 Term.** This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated. Either Party may terminate it upon **[INSERT NUMBER, e.g., thirty (30)]** days' written notice to the other.

**6.2 Survival of Confidentiality Obligations.** Termination or expiration does not relieve either Party of its obligations with respect to Confidential Information disclosed before termination. Specifically:

**(a) General Confidential Information.** The obligations in Sections 2 and 3 survive for **[INSERT PERIOD — RECOMMEND five (5)]** years following the date of disclosure of the applicable Confidential Information.

**(b) Trade Secrets.** With respect to any Confidential Information constituting a trade secret under applicable law, the obligations in Sections 2 and 3 survive **for so long as such information remains a trade secret** under applicable law, without time limit.

**(c) Personal and Financial Data.** With respect to Confidential Information described in Section 1.2(f), the obligations survive **indefinitely**.

**6.3 Surviving Sections.** Sections 1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, and 13 survive any termination or expiration of this Agreement.

## 7. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION OF MATERIALS

**7.1 Obligation.** Upon the earlier of (a) the Disclosing Party's written request at any time, or (b) termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly, and in any event within **[INSERT NUMBER, e.g., ten (10)]** business days, at the Disclosing Party's election, return or destroy all Confidential Information in its possession or control, including all copies, notes, analyses, and derivative materials, in every form and medium.

**7.2 Certification.** Upon request, an authorized officer of the Receiving Party shall certify in writing that it has complied with Section 7.1.

**7.3 Retained Copies.** The Receiving Party may retain (a) one archival copy solely to the extent required to comply with applicable law, regulation, or bona fide internal document-retention or compliance policy, and (b) copies existing solely in routine, automated backup systems not readily accessible in the ordinary course. Any retained copy remains subject to this Agreement's confidentiality and non-use obligations **for as long as it is retained**, notwithstanding Section 6.2(a).

## 8. NO REPRESENTATIONS; NO OBLIGATION TO PROCEED

**8.1 "AS IS."** All Confidential Information is provided **"AS IS."** Neither Party makes any representation or warranty, express or implied, as to the accuracy, completeness, or fitness for any purpose of any Confidential Information, and neither Party shall have any liability to the other resulting from the other's use of or reliance upon it. This Section does not limit any representation or warranty made in a subsequent definitive written agreement between the Parties.

**8.2 No Obligation to Disclose or Proceed.** Neither Party is obligated to disclose any particular information or to proceed with any transaction or relationship. This Agreement does not create any joint venture, partnership, agency, employment, or fiduciary relationship between the Parties.

**8.3 Independent Development by Either Party.** Subject at all times to Sections 2 and 3, nothing in this Agreement restricts either Party from independently developing, acquiring, or marketing any product or service, provided it does so without use of, reference to, or reliance upon the other Party's Confidential Information.

## 9. REMEDIES

**9.1 Irreparable Harm.** Each Party acknowledges that the Confidential Information is unique and valuable, that a breach or threatened breach of Sections 2 or 3 would cause the Disclosing Party irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy, and that the extent of such harm would be difficult to ascertain.

**9.2 Injunctive Relief.** Accordingly, in the event of a breach or threatened breach of Sections 2 or 3, the Disclosing Party is entitled to seek temporary, preliminary, and permanent injunctive relief and specific performance, in addition to any other remedy available at law or in equity. **[BRACKETED — REVIEW WITH COUNSEL: To the fullest extent permitted by applicable law, the Receiving Party waives any requirement that the Disclosing Party post a bond or other security in connection with such relief, and waives any defense that the Disclosing Party has an adequate remedy at law.]**

**9.3 Cumulative Remedies.** All remedies are cumulative and not exclusive. Nothing in this Agreement limits either Party's rights or remedies under the Defend Trade Secrets Act, the **[INSERT STATE]** Uniform Trade Secrets Act, the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act, or any other applicable law.

**9.4 Attorneys' Fees.** In any action to enforce this Agreement, the prevailing Party is entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.

**9.5 No Limitation of Liability.** Neither Party's liability for breach of Sections 2 or 3, or for misappropriation of the other's trade secrets, is subject to any limitation or exclusion of liability, whether set forth in this Agreement or in any other agreement between the Parties, unless expressly stated otherwise in a later written agreement signed by both Parties that references this Section by number.

## 11. GENERAL PROVISIONS

**11.1 Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties concerning its subject matter and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous understandings, whether oral or written, concerning that subject matter.

**11.2 Amendment.** This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

**11.3 Waiver.** No waiver of any provision is effective unless in writing and signed by the waiving Party. No failure or delay in exercising any right operates as a waiver of it, and no single or partial exercise precludes any further exercise.

**11.4 Severability and Reformation.** If any provision is held invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, it shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable while preserving the Parties' intent to the greatest degree permitted, and the remaining provisions remain in full force and effect. If it cannot be so modified, it shall be severed.

**11.5 Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the other's prior written consent, except that either Party may assign it without consent to a successor in connection with a merger, reorganization, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets or of the business to which this Agreement relates. Any purported assignment in violation of this Section is void. This Agreement binds and inures to the benefit of the Parties and their permitted successors and assigns.

**11.6 Notices.** All notices must be in writing and delivered to the address in the preamble by (a) personal delivery, (b) nationally recognized overnight courier, or (c) email to **[INSERT EMAIL ADDRESSES]** with confirmation of transmission. Notice is effective upon receipt.

**11.7 Counterparts and Electronic Signature.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which is an original and all of which together constitute one instrument. Signatures delivered electronically or by facsimile, including via a recognized electronic-signature platform, have the same effect as original signatures.

**11.8 Construction.** This Agreement is the product of negotiation between the Parties and shall not be construed against either Party as the drafter. Headings are for convenience only and do not affect interpretation. "Including" means "including without limitation."

**11.9 Third-Party Beneficiaries.** There are no third-party beneficiaries to this Agreement, except that each Party's Representatives and permitted assigns are intended beneficiaries of the protections afforded to that Party.

## 12. DATA PROTECTION AND FINANCIAL PRIVACY

**12.1 No Personal Data Absent a Separate Agreement.** The Parties do not intend to exchange personal data, consumer financial information, or production customer data under this Agreement. Neither Party shall disclose such data to the other except pursuant to a separate written data-processing or data-sharing agreement executed by both Parties.

**12.2 Inadvertent Disclosure.** If either Party inadvertently receives such data, it shall promptly notify the Disclosing Party, shall not access, use, or further disclose it, and shall return or securely delete it at the Disclosing Party's direction.

**12.3 Compliance.** To the extent either Party lawfully processes such data in connection with the Purpose, it shall do so in compliance with all applicable data-protection and financial-privacy laws, including the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act and its implementing regulations, applicable state financial-privacy and data-breach-notification laws, and, where applicable, the California Consumer Privacy Act and the EU/UK General Data Protection Regulation.
